# Workflow: Discovery Interview Conduct

**Track:** 2 — Audit
**Trigger:** You are ON the audit call with a prospect
**Input:** `[company_name]_audit_package.md` — open this before and during the call
**Output:** `[company_name]_interview_notes.md` — raw notes captured during/after call

---

## Objective

Run the audit discovery call in a way that surfaces genuine pain points, builds deep trust, and positions the $10K audit as the obvious next step. You are an inefficiency detective, not a salesperson.

---

## Before the Call (5 min)

- [ ] Open `[company_name]_audit_package.md`
- [ ] Review company summary and your contact's name/role
- [ ] Read your top 3 hypotheses
- [ ] Set up screen recording or Fireflies.ai for transcription (with permission)
- [ ] Have blank doc ready for notes

---

## Call Structure

### [00:00–05:00] Open — Set the Tone

**Script:**
> "Thanks for making time. Quick agenda for today: I want to spend most of this call understanding your business — how things work, where the friction is, what's taking more time than it should. No pitching, no selling. At the end, I'll share what I'm seeing and we can decide together if it makes sense to go deeper. Sound good?"

**Why this works:** Sets you as a peer/advisor, removes sales pressure, they relax and open up.

**Then ask:**
> "Before we dive in — can you give me a 2-minute overview of what [Company] does and your role in it?"

Listen for: their own language, how they describe their business, what they lead with (usually what they're proud of or worried about).

---

### [05:00–25:00] Stakeholder Discovery

Work through your customized questions from the audit package. Do NOT read them robotically — use them as a guide.

**Core question sequence:**

1. **Role & Team**
   > "Walk me through how your team is structured and what you're responsible for."

2. **Core Processes**
   > "What are the most critical processes your team runs — the ones where if they break, everything breaks?"

3. **The Bottleneck Probe**
   > "Where do things slow down or get stuck the most?"
   
   Follow up: "How long has that been the case? Have you tried fixing it?"

4. **The Time Question**
   > "If I looked at how your team spends time, what would surprise me about how much goes to [manual / repetitive / admin] work?"

5. **The Magic Wand**
   > "If you could fix one thing in your operations overnight — just wave a wand — what would it be?"

6. **Tools & Systems**
   > "Walk me through the main tools your team uses day to day. Where do things fall through the cracks between systems?"

**Golden technique — "Tell me more":**
When they mention a pain point, don't move on. Dig:
> "Tell me more about that."
> "What does that look like day to day?"
> "What happens when [that] goes wrong?"
> "How many people are involved in that?"
> "How long does that take?"

---

### [25:00–40:00] Deep Dive — Best Hypothesis

Pick the strongest pain signal from the last 20 minutes. Double-click on it.

**Structure:**
1. Describe the current process step by step
2. Identify every manual step
3. Quantify: how many people, how many hours, how often
4. Ask about the cost: "If that step took half the time, what would your team do with those hours?"

**Capture verbatim:**
Write down the exact words they use to describe their pain. These become the language in your proposal.

---

### [40:00–48:00] The Pivot — Offer the Audit

**Transition:**
> "Based on what you've told me, I'm seeing 2–3 areas where AI could make a significant difference — [brief summary]. What I'd like to propose is this: rather than guessing where to start, we do a proper audit first."

**Explain the audit:**
> "Here's what that looks like: over 2 weeks, I come in and interview [3–5 / 10–15] people at different levels of your business. We map out your core processes visually — you get a diagram of how everything actually works today. Then we build an opportunity map — every AI solution that could help, prioritized by impact and how easy they are to implement. At the end, you get a presentation with exactly what to build, in what order, with a clear ROI calculation for each. Even if you decide not to work with me after that, you'll have a strategic roadmap you can take anywhere."

**Price:**
> "For a business your size, that's [X]. Most clients see at least [5–10x] return in savings within the first year."

---

### [48:00–55:00] Close + Next Steps

**If they seem interested:**
> "Does that kind of process make sense for where you are right now?"
> [Wait for response]
> "What questions do you have about how it works?"

**Handle objections (see `objection_handling.md`)**

**Close:**
> "If you want to move forward, next step is simple — I send you a short agreement and we book the first interview for [date]. You'd have your full presentation within 2 weeks. Want me to send that over?"

---

## After the Call (15 min)

Immediately while memory is fresh:
1. Write raw notes to `.tmp/[company_name]_interview_notes.md`
2. Note: exact words they used for their pain, strongest pain points, their budget signals, objections raised
3. Rate call: Hot / Warm / Cold
4. Next action: send follow-up, proposal, or nurture

**Follow-up email (within 1 hour):**
> "Great talking with you today. As discussed, here's a summary of what we covered: [2–3 bullet points of their specific pains]. I'll send over the audit proposal by [tomorrow / date]. Looking forward to it."

---

## Notes Quality Standard

Good notes contain:
- Their EXACT words for their problems (not your paraphrase)
- Specific numbers where possible (X hours/week, Y people, Z times/month)
- The moment that felt like a breakthrough (usually where they got animated)
- Any objections or hesitations they raised
