# Alex Hormozi — $100M Offers Framework

Source: $100M Offers book / acquisition.com content

---

## The Value Equation

```
Value = (Dream Outcome × Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)
```

To increase value:
- Increase: Dream Outcome, Likelihood of Achievement
- Decrease: Time Delay, Effort & Sacrifice

---

## The Grand Slam Offer

A Grand Slam Offer is so good that prospects feel stupid saying no.

### Components

**1. Dream Outcome**
What the customer actually wants (not your product feature).
Define it in their words. "Double your revenue", "Cut admin time in half".

**2. Likelihood of Achievement**
Credibility + proof that it's actually possible.
Case studies, specific numbers, named clients.

**3. Time to Value**
How fast they see results. Faster = higher perceived value.
"Results in 2 weeks" beats "results in 3 months".

**4. Effort & Sacrifice**
How much work does the CLIENT have to do?
Lower the effort for them. "We handle everything" is a value multiplier.

**5. Value Stack**
Core offer + primary benefit + proof + guarantee + bonuses.
Itemize each element with its standalone value.

**6. Guarantee**
Removes purchase risk. Types:
- Unconditional money-back (strongest)
- Results-based (refund if no results)
- Partial refund (customer pays X%, keeps Y%)
The more specific the guarantee, the stronger the offer.

**7. Bonuses**
Add-ons that increase perceived value without proportional cost.
Must be relevant, desirable, ideally things they'd pay for separately.

**8. Urgency & Scarcity**
Deadline-based (limited time) or quantity-based (limited spots).
Both increase conversion. Must be real, not manufactured.

## Pricing Architecture

- Charge based on VALUE DELIVERED, not your costs
- Price signals quality — cheap = low trust for premium services
- For AI agency: price = % of value you create (if you save $200K/year, charge $30K–$60K)
- Use tiered pricing only when you have clearly differentiated service levels

---

## Offer Scoring

Score your offer against the value equation. For each element, ask:
- Dream Outcome: Is it specific, compelling, in their language? (1–10)
- Likelihood: Do you have proof? Is it believable? (1–10)
- Time: How fast do they see results? (1–10, 10 = immediate)
- Effort: How easy is it for them? (1–10, 10 = zero effort)

Total score out of 40. Below 30 = weak offer, fix before outreach.

## For AI Agency Context

Apply the framework to your AI audit offer:
- **Dream Outcome**: "Know exactly which AI projects will make/save you the most money, in what order to build them"
- **Likelihood**: "We've mapped [X] businesses. Here's what we found for a company like yours"
- **Time**: "2-week audit, results delivered in a single presentation"
- **Effort**: "You do the interviews. We do all the research, mapping, and analysis"
- **Guarantee**: "If you don't identify at least $50K in annual savings, full refund"
- **Bonus**: "We'll also identify the #1 Quick Win you can implement yourself in under a week"
