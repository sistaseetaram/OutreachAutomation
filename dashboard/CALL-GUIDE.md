# Live Call Guide — Free Diagnosis Call

Run this on every live call with a studio owner. Adapted from the Liam Ottley AI-audit
blueprint + the discovery-interview workflow, scaled down to one owner, one call, one free build.

## The one rule

You are an **inefficiency detective, not a salesperson.** 80/20 — they talk 80%, you talk 20%.
You are not here to pitch. You're here to understand how they run live projects and find where it
leaks. Do this right and **they sell themselves.**

## Before you dial (2 min)

- Open their thread card. Re-read the hook, firm, recent project.
- Have one genuine specific ready about their work.
- Note your 2–3 guesses at their biggest pain (from the 6 leak points below).
- Blank notes doc open — you will capture their EXACT words.
- If recording, ask permission at the top.

---

## The call — what to do, in order

### ▶︎ The second they pick up  [0:00–0:30]  — disarm
Warm, not stiff. Thank them. Set the no-pitch frame **immediately**:
> Hey [Name], thanks for hopping on. Quick agenda — I mostly want to understand how your studio runs its live projects and where things get hectic. No pitch, no selling. At the end I'll tell you honestly what I'm seeing and we decide together if there's anything worth doing. Cool?

### ▶︎ Get them talking about THEIR work  [0:30–3:00]  — rapport
Lead with the genuine specific:
> Before anything — I really liked [specific: Café Kaadhale's zoning / the RAAGA villas / their recent project]. Can you tell me a bit about the studio and what you're focused on right now?

Let them run. Listen for what they lead with (pride or worry). Match their tone.

### ▶︎ Discovery — the audit, as a conversation  [3:00–20:00]
Walk the live-project lifecycle. Ask about PROBLEMS, never pitch solutions. Anchor on the **6 places studios leak**:
> 1. Site updates — "Once a project's on site, how do updates actually reach you — WhatsApp, calls, site visits?"
> 2. Revisions — "How do you track client changes? Ever lose one or end up redoing work?"
> 3. Approvals — "What's pending-approval tracking like? Anything ever stall waiting on a sign-off?"
> 4. Vendor blockers — "Where do vendors/contractors slow you down? Do you find out before or after it's a problem?"
> 5. Follow-ups — "When you're juggling a few sites at once, what falls through the cracks?"
> 6. Payments / scope — "How do you track payment milestones and extra scope? Ever do work that didn't get billed?"

The golden technique — when they name a pain, DON'T move on. Dig:
> Tell me more about that.
> What does that look like on a bad week?
> What happens when that slips?
> How often does that happen?
> How much time does chasing that eat?

The magic-wand question:
> If you could fix one thing about running projects overnight — just wave a wand — what would it be?

Quantify quietly: how many projects at once, how many people, hours/week chasing updates, the value of one slipped payment. The numbers will sell for you later.

### ▶︎ Mirror back — this IS the audit  [20:00–28:00]
Reflect their pain in THEIR words. No solutions yet. Make them feel seen:
> So if I heard you right — [pain 1 in their words], and the part that really costs you is [pain 2]. When [X] slips, it ends up [their consequence]. Did I get that right?

Let them confirm and add more. This is where trust is won — you diagnosed, you didn't prescribe.

### ▶︎ The pivot — only if the pain is real  [28:00–34:00]
If there's no real pain, DON'T pitch:
> Honestly, sounds like you've got this dialed — I'm not going to sell you something you don't need.

(That builds more trust than any pitch, and they remember you.)

If pain IS real, go **"built, not advised"** — offer to SHOW, not tell:
> Here's what I'd suggest — instead of me telling you what could help, let me just build it. Pick one live project. Over the next 7 days I'll set up a simple daily control view for it — site updates, pending approvals, what's blocked, who you're waiting on, payment milestones — built around your existing WhatsApp and sheets, nothing new for your team to learn. Free. You keep it either way. If it's useful we talk about doing it properly across projects; if not, no harm. Fair?

### ▶︎ Close — get the ONE input  [34:00–40:00]
Don't end vague — that's exactly how Studio Artha stalled. Get the concrete thing:
> Great — to start I just need one thing: point me at one live project and forward me the last few days of its site/WhatsApp updates. I'll have a first version back to you in a few days. Can you send that across today or tomorrow?

Lock the next contact: "I'll Loom you a walkthrough on [day]." Write it into the thread + ClickUp before you do anything else.

## How to audit WITHOUT selling (the core of it)

1. **Diagnose, don't prescribe.** Find pain on the call. Don't propose solutions live.
2. **Ask, don't tell.** Every solution-thought → flip it into a question.
3. **Mirror their words.** Repeat their pain back in their language. Feeling understood beats being impressed.
4. **Let numbers sell.** Quantify the pain; the cost makes the case, not you.
5. **Built, not advised.** Offer to SHOW a working thing, not to pitch a service.
6. **The takeaway stands alone.** The free dashboard has value even if they never pay — no strings, no sales pressure.
7. **Be willing to walk.** "You don't need this" is the most disarming, trust-building sentence you have. Use it when it's true.
8. **Permission language.** "Cool?" "Fair?" "Did I get that right?" — they stay in control, they stay open.

## Common points to hit EVERY call (checklist)

- [ ] No-pitch frame set in the first 30 seconds
- [ ] Opened with one genuine specific about their work
- [ ] They talked ~80% of the time
- [ ] Walked all 6 leak points (updates, revisions, approvals, vendors, follow-ups, payments)
- [ ] Used "tell me more" at least 3 times
- [ ] Asked the magic-wand question
- [ ] Captured at least 3 pains in THEIR exact words
- [ ] Quantified at least one pain (time or money)
- [ ] Mirrored their pain back and got a "yes, exactly"
- [ ] Either offered the free 7-day build OR honestly said they don't need it
- [ ] Left with ONE concrete next step + a date — never "let's schedule sometime"
- [ ] Logged it in the thread + ClickUp within 15 min

## Objection handling (studio-specific)

| They say | You say |
|---|---|
| "Is this AI?" | "Some of it, but only for coordination and documentation — never design. You and your judgment stay fully in the loop." |
| "We don't need AI for design." | "Completely agree — I'm not touching design. I look at the work *around* design: site coordination, approvals, follow-ups, payments." |
| "Clients trust me / I like being hands-on." | "That's exactly why this works — it gives *you* more visibility, it doesn't put tech between you and your client. You stay hands-on, just with less chasing." |
| "We already have a system." | "Nice — what do you use? And where does it still leak — what do you still end up chasing on WhatsApp?" (Find the gap, don't argue.) |
| "I don't have time for this." | "That's the point — 7 days, free, zero new tools for your team. I do the work, you just point me at one project." |
| "Send me details / not now." | "Totally — can I send a 2-min Loom instead of a doc? And if now's not right, when should I check back — in 2 weeks?" (Pin a real time.) |

## After the call (15 min, while it's fresh)

- Write their EXACT pain words into the thread log.
- Rate the call: hot / warm / cold.
- Update `status` + `next_action` + `next_action_due` in the thread; mirror the stage in ClickUp.
- If you offered the free build: status → `call-booked`; next = "await their one input / start the build."
- Send a 1-line recap within the hour:
> Great talking — you mentioned [pain 1] and [pain 2]. Send me [the one project's updates] and I'll have a first version back by [day].
