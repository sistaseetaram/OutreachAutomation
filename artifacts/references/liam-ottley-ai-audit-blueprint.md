Source: Liam Ottley, AAA Accelerator / Morningside AI
Framework: 3-Step AI Audit → $10K starting price, increase after positive feedback

## The Core Idea

Pure AI development value is dropping fast. Agencies that win are those who provide strategic guidance — become an AITP (AI Transformation Partner), not just a developer.

An AI audit is the entry product: 2 weeks, $10K, delivers a strategic roadmap. It naturally leads to larger implementation projects.

## Step 1: Discovery Interviews

**Goal:** Become an "inefficiency detective." Find friction, repetitive tasks, manual processes. DO NOT suggest solutions yet.

**Who to interview:**
- Leadership/stakeholders (30,000-ft view) — goals, bottlenecks, KPIs
- End-users/employees (ground reality) — daily tasks, actual frustrations

**Format:**
- 30–45 min per interview
- 3–5 interviews for small business (10–50 employees)
- Up to 10–15 for larger/complex orgs
- Remote or in-person both work
- Record with permission (Fireflies.ai for transcription)

**Golden rules:**
- 80/20 split — they talk, you listen
- Ask "Why?" repeatedly — get to root cause
- Focus on PROBLEMS not solutions during interviews

### Stakeholder Interview Questions

**Role & Team:**
- Describe your role and team's primary responsibilities
- What are your main KPIs this quarter/year?
- Walk me through your team structure

**Core Processes:**
- What are the most critical processes your team manages?
- Where are the biggest bottlenecks or delays?
- Which tasks consume the most man-hours?

**Tools & Tech:**
- What software does your team rely on?
- What are your biggest frustrations with current tech?
- What processes happen outside main software (spreadsheets, email, manual docs)?

**Pain Points:**
- Biggest challenges your team faces right now?
- Magic wand: one problem you'd solve overnight?
- What prevents your team from being more efficient?

**Future Vision:**
- Where do you see the biggest improvement opportunities?
- How does your team respond to new technology?

### End-User Interview Questions

**Daily Role:**
- Walk me through a typical day/week in your role
- What are the 1–3 most common tasks you do every day?
- How much time is spent on core work vs. admin/repetitive tasks?

**Process Deep Dive:**
- Walk me through the exact steps to complete [specific common task]
- Which part of that process is most manual or time-consuming?
- What information do you need to find to complete this task, and where do you get it?

**Tools & Frustrations:**
- What software do you spend most of your day in?
- What's most frustrating about your current tools?
- Is there double-entry of data or copy-pasting between systems?

**Pain Points & Wishlist:**
- What's the most boring or repetitive part of your job?
- If you had an assistant, what tasks would you give them immediately?
- How do you track your work or report on progress?

## Step 2: Map the Process & Find Opportunities

### Part A: Ops Canvas

Map the business into 3 fundamental engines:
1. **Acquisition Engine** — how they find and sign new customers
2. **Delivery Engine** — how they deliver their product/service
3. **Support Engine** — how they handle post-sale customer issues

Tag each step with friction indicators:
- 🟡 **Time Sinks** — highly manual, repetitive, consumes employee time
- 🟡 **Quality Risks** — prone to human error or inconsistencies

Tools: Miro, FigJam, or **Excalidraw** (preferred)

### Part B: AI Opportunity Matrix

For each friction point, identify a potential AI solution. Plot on 2x2 grid:
- X-axis: Business Impact (low → high)
- Y-axis: Implementation Effort (low → high)

**4 Quadrants:**
1. **Quick Wins** ⭐ — Low effort, high impact. Build these FIRST. Proves value fast.
2. **Big Swings** 🚀 — High effort, high impact. Long-term transformation projects.
3. **Nice-to-Haves** 👍 — Low effort, low impact. Add-ons only.
4. **Deprioritize** 🗑️ — High effort, low impact. Actively avoid these.

### Part C: Validate with Client

Run a validation workshop with key stakeholder(s):
- Frame as collaborative, not a declaration
- Key questions: "Which Quick Wins resonate most?", "Are there hidden complexities we missed?", "Does this align with your 6–12 month priorities?"
- Goal: co-create the prioritized list together → builds trust

## Step 3: Present Findings — The 5 Key Slides

### Slide 1: Scope & Objectives
- Restate project goals
- Show you understood their core needs

### Slide 2: Opportunity Matrix
- Present the full 2x2 grid
- All potential projects categorized

### Slide 3: Roadmap Summary
- Timeline view of prioritized opportunities
- Phase 1 (Quick Wins) → Phase 2 (Big Swings)

### Slide 4: Opportunity Deep Dive
- For each top 1–3 Quick Wins: Current State vs. Future State diagram
- Show time saved per process

### Slide 5: ROI Summary — The Money Slide
- Table: AI solution | implementation cost | annual savings | ROI %
- This is what gets buy-in

## ROI Calculator Formula

### Direct Cost Savings
```
Hours saved/week = (Time on task/week) × (Staff doing task) × (% time AI saves)
Annual savings = (Hours saved/week) × (Avg hourly rate) × 52
ROI% = (Annual savings / Implementation cost) × 100

Avg hourly rate = Annual salary / 2080
```

### Revenue Uplift (advanced)
```
Revenue-generating hours unlocked = Hours saved × 50%
Additional weekly revenue = Unlocked hours × Value per hour
Potential additional annual revenue = Additional weekly × 52
```
