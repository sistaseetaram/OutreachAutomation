# Outreach Loom — 90-second script structure

A sales arc, not a tutorial. Three beats, ≤150 words spoken total.

## Beat 1 — Observation (0:00–0:15)
- ONE specific, real thing about their studio/work (from the research profile or their IG).
- Why it caught your eye. No flattery, no hype.
- Example: "Saw your Jaipur courtyard villa reveal — the way you phased the site updates stood out."

## Beat 2 — One concrete help (0:15–0:70)
- Name the boring/manual task in THEIR words (quotes between sheets, specs by hand, status reports).
- The ONE buildbridge output you'd make them free (mood board / overlay / material-lighting-BOQ sheet / FF&E).
- The outcome, conservative + measured: "this takes your team ~X hours; we bring it close to zero —
  I'll measure it on one real project so it's honest."
- On screen: a real sample of that output beside their manual version.

## Beat 3 — Soft CTA (0:70–0:90)
- Offer the free diagnosis / first build. Low pressure, optional.
- Write the close line VERBATIM and stop. Example: "If that's useful, send me one project's
  last update and I'll show you the mapped version this week."

## Rules
- ONE offer, never a menu. ≤90s. "Built, not advised" — point at something real.
- Setu voice (see `tools/setu_voice.py`); 0 forbidden words. Never fabricate the observation.
- Match the offer to segment: interior → buildbridge stages; architecture/construction → doc/report
  automation (status reports, BOQ).
