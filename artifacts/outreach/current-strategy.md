# Current Outreach Strategy

Date locked: 2026-06-07
Channel: LinkedIn (standard search — no Sales Navigator account)
ICP: Architecture and interior studio founders/principals in Bangalore and Hyderabad

## Decision

Lead with studio workflow systems for architecture and interior firms.

Do not lead with AI design automation.

AI is the implementation layer, not the headline.

## Why This Is Locked

Owner and firm-leader evidence shows architects are curious about AI, but protective of design authorship, liability, client trust, IP, and professional judgment.

Market lesson:

- Architects are not anti-AI.
- Architects resist "AI replacing design judgment."
- Owners respond better to project control, coordination relief, admin reduction, and margin protection.

## Primary Offer

One live project, 14-day Project Control Room.

It turns existing WhatsApp/site updates, revisions, approvals, vendor blockers, billing triggers, and payment milestones into:

- daily owner dashboard
- morning summary
- action/blocker tracker
- pending approval list
- site/vendor follow-up list
- scope/payment leakage alerts

Promise:

- reduce owner status-chasing
- catch site blockers earlier
- reduce missed follow-ups
- reduce revision/payment leakage
- improve owner visibility without asking team to adopt a heavy new tool

## Campaign Positioning

Use:

> I help architecture/interior studio owners get more control over active projects by systemizing site updates, revisions, approvals, documentation, follow-ups, and payment milestones.

Avoid:

- AI design automation
- AI-powered architecture firm
- replace junior designers
- automate your creative process
- CRM for architects
- productivity dashboard
- digital transformation
- generic AI audit

## First Message Frame

```text
Hi [Name], I am studying how architecture/interior studio owners keep control over active projects once work moves to site.

Mostly site updates, revisions, approvals, vendor blockers, documentation, follow-ups, and payment milestones.

I am testing a 14-day project control room with Nine Bricks now. It works around existing WhatsApp/Sheets flow, not a new app rollout.

Open to a quick 15-min chat so I can understand how your studio handles this?
```

## Sharper Pain Hook

Use when profile suggests active execution/site work:

```text
Are you still finding out site blockers, client changes, vendor delays, or unpaid scope from WhatsApp calls after the damage is done?
```

Then:

```text
I am testing a 14-day control room for one active project. It turns site updates, revisions, approvals, vendor/payment blockers, and billing milestones into one daily owner view.

No new app rollout for your team. I build it around your current WhatsApp/Sheets flow.
```

## Design Automation Rule

Default:

- Do not mention design automation in first touch.
- Do not sell AI-generated concepts, mood boards, FF&E, or BOQ decisions upfront.
- Do not imply AI can understand client/architect taste better than architect.

Exception:

- Mention design support only if the lead clearly posts about AI, design technology, visualization, parametric workflows, or tech-led practice.

Allowed wording:

```text
If design support is useful, I can explore it too, but only as support for your direction. Main focus is reducing coordination/admin load around active projects.
```

## Response To "Is This AI?"

```text
Some parts can use AI, but only for coordination and documentation support.

Not for replacing design judgment. Owner/principal review stays in the loop.
```

## Response To "We Do Not Need AI For Design"

```text
I agree. I am not trying to automate design taste.

I am looking at the work around design: site coordination, revision tracking, documentation, follow-ups, and owner visibility.
```

## ICP Priority

Best-fit leads:

- principal architect / founder / partner / studio owner
- 3-25 person architecture or interior studio
- active residential, commercial, workplace, hospitality, or design-and-build projects
- visible execution/site coordination responsibility
- likely using WhatsApp, calls, Sheets, PDFs, and scattered documents
- founder still involved in project follow-up

**Secondary segment (validated via Raju, 2026-06-08):**
Senior/established architects who are NOT owners but have high influence and daily design output.
- 15+ years experience, multiple project types (villas, offices, function halls, malls)
- Do pre-commitment design work (1–2 renders/drawings before client pays advance) — this is unpaid time and a real pain
- AI-aware: already know tools like 3ds Max AI plugins; not scared, just waiting for someone to build for them
- Want personalized output trained on their own past work — not generic AI style
- Hard requirement: measurement-accurate renders from actual 2D plans
- Path to budget: need intro to firm owner/contractor above them
- Pitch angle: "stop doing unpaid design work — let the system give you the first draft before the client commits"

Lower priority:

- pure conceptual studios with low execution involvement
- large firms with mature PM/BIM ops
- designers posting mostly aesthetic/craft philosophy with no operations signal
- students, employees, junior architects with no influence

- KKD Studio founder: AI improves client communication, reporting, scheduling, document parsing; not automating design. https://architectureupdate.in/human-centric-design-will-always-have-an-edge-ai-is-the-silent-workhorse/
